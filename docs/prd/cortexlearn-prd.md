# CortexLearn AI - Founder-Grade PRD

## Product name
CortexLearn AI

## One-line description
An AI learning intelligence platform that figures out how you're taught, tested, and trained, then turns that into mastery tools.

## Problem
Most study and training tools do one of three things badly:
- They summarize content without prioritizing what actually matters.
- They generate flashcards without understanding how the instructor or organization evaluates people.
- They ignore visual and contextual signals like diagrams, screenshots, SOP flowcharts, repeated phrasing, and quiz patterns.

Users do not need more notes. They need:
- Prioritization
- Likely assessment prediction
- Pattern detection
- Evidence-backed review tools
- Adaptive practice

## Core insight
The highest-value knowledge is not just what exists in the material. It is what the source authority emphasizes, repeats, tests, contrasts, and uses in practice.

## Product thesis
If CortexLearn AI can infer:
- What matters most
- How it is taught
- How it is tested
- What visual materials reinforce it
- Where the user is weak

Then it can generate much more useful study and workforce performance assets than generic note tools.

## Target users
Primary:
- College students
- Certification candidates
- Workforce learners in onboarding/training
- Compliance learners
- Technical enablement learners

Secondary:
- Managers and enablement leaders
- Professional continuing education users
- Internal training teams

## Use cases
- Convert a college course into exam-focused study cards.
- Turn nursing/pharmacology materials into mechanism + side effect review.
- Turn onboarding docs and CRM screenshots into role-ramp memory aids.
- Convert SOPs and process maps into error-prevention quick recall tools.
- Turn certification blueprints and mock exams into domain-weighted practice packs.

## Jobs to be done
- "Help me figure out what actually matters."
- "Turn all this material into high-yield review."
- "Show me how this instructor asks questions."
- "Use images and diagrams too, not just text."
- "Tell me what I'm weak on and what to review next."
- "Give me evidence so I can trust the output."

## Core value proposition
Upload your materials. CortexLearn AI learns how the material is taught and evaluated, then builds evidence-backed mastery tools.

## User promise
"CortexLearn doesn't just summarize your materials. It learns the pattern behind what matters."

## MVP goals
A user should be able to:
- Create a learning space
- Upload primary and supporting material
- See detected topics/patterns
- Generate evidence-backed study cards
- Take a practice quiz
- Review weak areas
- Use image-based materials as part of the workflow

## Non-goals for MVP
- Live lecture recording
- Full LMS sync
- Real-time collaboration
- Production-grade prosody analysis
- Fully automated spaced repetition scheduling
- Enterprise admin console

## Core product pillars
1. Learning DNA: Extract structure from syllabi, blueprints, agendas, roadmaps.
2. Pattern Intelligence: Detect teaching style, quiz style, emphasis, repeated terms, warnings, common mistakes.
3. Evidence-Backed Generation: Every card, quiz, and summary should connect back to evidence.
4. Adaptive Mastery: Track quiz attempts and identify weak areas.
5. Visual Intelligence: Treat diagrams, screenshots, flowcharts, and scanned pages as first-class learning inputs.

## Key screens
- Dashboard
- Learning Spaces
- Learning Space Detail
- Materials Hub
- Analysis Dashboard
- Card Studio
- Practice Center
- Evidence View
- Export Center
- Settings

## Success metrics
Activation:
- Time to first generated study card
- Percent of users who upload at least 2 materials
- Percent of users who run analysis after upload

Engagement:
- Cards generated per learning space
- Quizzes generated per learning space
- Repeat sessions per week
- Evidence view opens per session

Retention:
- Users returning to same learning space
- Quiz retry rate
- Weak area review completion rate

Product quality:
- Percent of cards with evidence attached
- Percent of generated outputs passing validation
- Percent of image uploads successfully OCR'd or classified

## Risks
- Low-quality OCR on images
- Weak mock analysis producing unbelievable outputs
- Overclaiming confidence
- Cluttered UX from too many features
- AI outputs feeling generic instead of source-specific

## Product principles
- Evidence first
- Teach pattern, not just content
- Prioritize what matters
- Visual inputs matter
- Never feel like a black box
- Be useful fast
